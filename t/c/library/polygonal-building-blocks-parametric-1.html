These snap together blocks combine to form complex shapes that are far more impressive than their simple demeanor. They reach far beyond building polyhedra, offering the possibility of endless combinations in both two and three dimensions. These renderings show only a small fraction of what is capable. Feel free to take a closer look at the included assemblies via GrabCAD 3D viewer.

Applications range from education to decoration to simply being a toy.

Children will be much more engaged when being taught geometry with these blocks (both 2D and 3D), as they are able to tangibly see and feel the geometry and visually experience the process of changing from one structure to another.

Spice up your desk by gluing together several blocks to make a permanent decoration.

Let your imagination run wild and use these blocks as a toy to create a plethora of interesting designs.

Being mostly flat, little to no support material is needed when printing these blocks. This increases the number of blocks which can printed with a single bottle, compared to more complex designs requiring much more support material.

Weights are as follows (PET Plastic):
-Triangle: 1.79g
-Square: 2.90g
-Pentagon: 3.84g
-Hexagon: 4.75g
-Heptagon: 5.63g
-Octagon: 6.51g

A single bottle will be easily able to print multiple parts.

Another advantage is that this design is fully parametric, meaning that you can change almost every facet of the design quickly and with ease. The ability to adjust these parameters to your needs lies within "Main Part.SLDPRT", open able in SolidWorks 2013.

Changeable parameters are:

-Side length
-Thickness
-Tolerance (distance between meshing surfaces, ensuring smooth -piece snapping when 3D printed)
-Number of Sides (can range from 3 to infinity. Sides 3 to 8 are included in the file, but it is possible to create as many sides as you wanted)
-Length of stock (the protruding arms)
-Width of stock
-Inside Offset (the offset from the outer edge to the inner cut-out)

Simple. Practical. Effective.
