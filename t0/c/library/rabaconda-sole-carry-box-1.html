Whew, I and another CADr just came up with this.

The design has standard parts: the hinge, the latch, extensible legs, fasteners (for the wall attachment). The overall design will be cheap.

Material: The box should be made of impact-resistant polypropylene or polyethylene, and for higher strength could be impregnated with fiber resins (prepegs or SMC). The bottom base (work surface) may be made out of wood, if need be.

Manufacture: It can be manufactured by extrusion, as this method has a medium-to-low tooling cost as well as a high production rate.

Mode of operation: The box, can be assembled on the ground, or hung on a wall, using the wall attachments (part as a CAD file). The legs are designed to be stored under the box (you might need to look under for the storage grooves :-) ). Also, the base is a working surface, nothing is stored in/on it. The top of the box opens other mini-compartments, in which goggles and spare parts can be stored. The middle of the box, has holes for attaching the legs, the legs also stop the bottom base from sliding backwards.
The handle is designed to stay out of the way of operation, and for easy carrying of the box; carrying the box this way keeps the arrangement (within the box) much the same way.

Note: Working on one more file... Contributions are welcome. :-)
