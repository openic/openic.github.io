Supported file types

  1. Fusion 360 Design
  2. Creo \ Pro/E (.prt, .asm)
    (Windows only)
  3. IGES (.igs, .iges)
  4. SolidWorks (.sldprt, .sldasm)
    (Windows only)
  5. STEP (.stp, .step)

System requirements

  1. an internet connection
  2. CPU: 64-bit processor (32-bit not supported)
  3. OS: Apple® Mac OS® X 10.7 or Microsoft® Windows® 7 or later
  4. memory: 3GB RAM (4GB recommended)
  5. hard drive space of approximately 300mb, for the Fusion 360 client
  6. a mouse or trackpad: Microsoft-compliant mouse, Apple Mouse, Magic Mouse, MacBook Pro trackpad, Space Mouse
  7. graphics card: all graphics cards on supported hardware (except Intel GMA X3100)

Description

Workbench users now have 3D design and editing tools available, making sophisticated CAD editing and collaboration tools accessible to users of all sizes and types.

Users are able to launch Fusion 360 from within Workbench and, after editing the model, can save the model back to Workbench.

Fusion 360 offers:

  • Powerful CAD editing software without leaving Workbench
  • Combine feature-based modelling with free-form sculpting
  • Save changes directly to private Workbench projects
  • Collaborate on changes in real time with both engineers and non-engineers
  • Access your designs almost anytime, anywhere from your Mac or PC

Notes:

  • Your Fusion 360 for GrabCAD plan is independent of your Workbench plan. You can use a paid Fusion plan with a Free Workbench plan and vice versa.

How to use

  1. Select "New Autodesk Fusion 360 Design" in your project files or click on "Open With" while viewing a 3D file to launch Fusion 360 for GrabCAD
  2. If you haven't already, install Fusion 360 for GrabCAD on your desktop
  3. Then create and edit CAD models. Simply save to your Workbench project when finished
