Help! Impossible flanged / mitered sheet metal seam in Solidworks .

SOLIDWORKS seam meet flanges miter sheet metal
By b_shell on 27 Jun 15:01 4 answers 20 views 2 comments

Ok, so it's probably not impossible, but I've spent a week on it and there has to be an easier way to model this. At issue is the area where the tank bottom piece meets the tank's side walls (the front end of the unit -see pics). Two flanges having different angles meet and then transition into a steeper angle before bending into a "lip". I'm constrained by the actual dimension of the physical tank that I'm modelling. I basically ended up brutishly hacking at it until the over all dimensions of the assembly were what they needed to be. I could really use some help.

    Ok after looking at your file I think T011A just needs to have the cut- extrude feature deleted and remade at the end of the assembly. this happens often and has more to do with the software, than anything else so just cut- extrude feature and re cut the part.

    1. Just-in about 5 hours ago

      I would use two pieces like you did for two reasons. One it really helps in nesting later. Secondly, it gives a frame so that bends on T401A will wrap arond the T011A as you most likely will never be able to produce the same exact bend every time (you will always be off a few degrees, due to different machines, and each sheet of metal has slightly different levels of softness) this way the metal can be pushed around the T011A. Knowing the average bend radius and kfactor of your metal and your machine is probley going to be really needed in this situation. so I would run a bend test on some scrap if you don't have a k factor and bend radius from your machine.

      This issue is very normal in my world. When you build a part I recommend using putting the cut tools as far back as possible. and if something doesn't look right you may need to just rebuild it, the software has a few registry issues but the company is working hard to resolve it.
