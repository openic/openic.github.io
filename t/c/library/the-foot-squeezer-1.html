Hello!

This is my entry for the Casabella "stringmop" competition.

The most important requirement here was besides ergonomic shape and functionality a different approach from those already used on the market.

As a ME I was thinking out of the box in a more mechanical way.
From my point of view it is easier on the back in standing position rather than bending over and this way doing the work. Also it is easier to use the leg muscles that are way stronger than arm muscles.
Combining these 2 elements I developed a completely new approach of squeezing out water.

My idea consists of an inner pully mechanism, a rope and a foot lever. It may sound very complicated but it really isn't. Rope is connecting the center element, on which the fibers are attached, and the foot lever, that is pressed down to squeeze out water. The pulley is only used to transfer the linerar motion of the foot pressing down the lever to the center element and by that lifting up the fibers, pushing them into the spherical shaped housing.

This is a completely new concept, I have never before seen anything like it and as far as being creative and developing a fully new concept, I think this idea based on those factors presents a hard competition.

Feedback is welcome!

    @Gavin Peters

    Thanks for your comment, your professor made a solid point. From my point of view the fibers would in the max. compressed position stick out like an inch or so, this way they wouldnt get clogged up inside the housing. Also another way of preventing the fibers to get stuck inside the housing would be using a slight cone shape, so the helix part would be a bit wider at the bottom than at the top. With a slight cone I mean like a degree or two. This way there would be an extra force produced by the housing while compressing the fibers inside and it would prevent the fibers to get stuck.
    As far as resistance goes, the resistance depends in the number of revolutions of the helix. The more revolutions, the more horizontal position take the fibers inside the housing and thus it is harder to get them outside. In my design I used 1 revolution in the total height, so the resistance would be really small. The whole helix conecept is really just an extra idea, for the sake of simplicity you could really use a straight shape (without helix) and this way there would be no resistance, but as I have written down, the helix shape has some good benefits and it surely is worthy of consideration.

    I hope I answered to your questions.
