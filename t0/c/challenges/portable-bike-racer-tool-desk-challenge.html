Portable Bike Racer Tool Desk Challenge

  • Description
  • Entries 76
  • Results

Rabaconda challenges you to create portable folding work desk for dirt bike riders and racing teams to be used at the track and garage for servicing the vehicles.

This challenge is about designing compact, portable and collapsible working desk, mainly focused for motorcycle riders needs, but could also be used in other areas.

The desk should be easy to set up, has holders for tools, spray cans and paper roll, drawer(s) for tools, small spare parts and goggles.

The desk can be used instead of tool case and has also clean working surface. It should have feet for setting up outdoors and also a possibility to easily fix on a wall.

Requirements

  • You must include:
    - STEP or IGES file

  • You are encouraged to include:
    - Native CAD file
    - Additional renderings

  • Design guidelines (UPDATED April 2):
    - Production-ready (medium sized batches)
    - Unit production cost around 80USD
    - Easy to setup
    - Lightweight
    - Rigid and stable
    - Dust and weatherproof when folded
    - Easily wall-mounted and locked

  • Recommended dimensions and specifications:
    - W: 800mm x D: 400mm x H: as slim as possible

    - minimum space between lid and surface: 45mm
    - minimum drawer height: 55mm
    - working surface height from ground: 800mm
    - universal mounts inside for most used tools
    (riders use same tools in different shapes)
    - drawer(s) for other tools and small spare parts
    (spark plug, brake pads etc.)
    - fixtures for two spray cans and a paper roll
    - pocket for goggles

  • **Check the photo, above, for common dirt bike riders tools. Upper level tools should be mounted on the lid, others in drawer(s).

  • Tõnu Kallast 3 months ago

    Hello everyone and thanks for practical questions! Keep them coming!

    The goal of this challenge is to have a “ready for production” solution.

    PRODUCTION COST
    This product is aimed for everybody who likes to have fun with bikes, karts, rc vehicles etc. and also likes to repair and service the vehicles by themselves. It should be an affordable alternative to a toolbox. So when it comes to production costs you should think how much an average enthusiast is willing to pay for your product and divide the price by 3 to get the production cost.
    We are aiming for a product that would be affordable for wide audience in its niche market. Depending on the design and features I would say that sale price between 100...200 USD/EUR would be reasonable. You are welcome to debate me.

    METHODS OF PRODUCTION
    I wouldn’t limit this down to certain materials. I assume the production cost will limit that. We plan to find a suitable manufacturer after choosing the best design that meets the requirements.

    DRAWER(S)
    The dimensions of the storage space for tools and spare parts should be approximately 550x400x55mm (WxDxH). 55mm is a minimum height. It would be practical to have some smaller compartments for spare parts so they don’t get mixed with bigger tools. Let’s say 3 at approx. 20x12cm would do it. It would be very nice if the tools won’t make much noise while transporting the case.

    Use of standard parts such as drawer sliders, hinges, handle etc. is favoured. It would be nice if you would refer to the source of standard elements in the description.

    Dimensions of goggles are roughly 200x100x95mm (Oakley Crowbar).

  • Chanson 3 months ago

    @Tõnu Kallast
    To answer your request for opinions on the reward, I would say it's all about balance. The current format of these challenges is pretty fair in my opinion, you offer your prize and it is up to us to determine if it is worth our time. If the pool of prizes is not very enticing, the quality of the entries and quantity will be poor, yet you will still need to pick a winner and award the prizes. If you offer a substantially better package, you will attract more entries and each individual will be more inclined to put effort in.

    I would not say there is a fair or unfair way about it, it's mainly concerned with improving your own position, especially because we are not required to participate but you are still required to award the prize. If you have demanding expectations and offer a pair of movie tickets as the prize, there is nothing wrong with that but you will probably not get many good entries.

    For the current proposal, I would use the challenge more as a screening process, offer a lower prize but with the offer of a contract to the winner to develop a further concept and offer higher compensation to go with that contract. As you said, you need to assure that the winner participates in redesigns/technical drawings etc, so you should not offer royalties until you know that the winner will cooperate with your needs. You wouldn't want to offer royalties to the winner, have them stop answering your emails two weeks later, then sue you two years later when you have a successful product that you had to hire and pay someone else to develop.
